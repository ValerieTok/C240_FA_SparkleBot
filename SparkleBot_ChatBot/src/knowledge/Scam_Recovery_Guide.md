# Scam Recovery Guide

Use this guide when a user says they have been scammed, lost money, clicked a suspicious link, installed a suspicious app, shared personal information, gave an OTP, or had an account taken over.

## Core response style

- Be calm, direct, and practical.
- Prioritise immediate loss prevention before explanation.
- Do not promise that money can be recovered.
- Ask only for the details needed to choose the next step.
- Never ask the user to share passwords, OTPs, full bank account numbers, NRIC, card numbers, or authentication codes in chat.
- If there is immediate danger or a crime in progress, tell the user to call 999.

## Immediate steps

1. Stop all contact with the scammer. Do not send more money, do not click more links, and do not install anything they send.
2. Contact the bank or card provider immediately if money, bank access, card details, Singpass, or payment apps may be involved. Ask about blocking cards, freezing accounts, reversing pending transactions, and activating anti-scam controls.
3. File a police report online or at the nearest Neighbourhood Police Centre or Neighbourhood Police Post. For urgent assistance in Singapore, call 999.
4. Secure affected accounts. Change passwords from a clean device, sign out other sessions, enable two-factor authentication, and recover email or social media accounts if they were taken over.
5. Save evidence. Keep screenshots, chat logs, phone numbers, usernames, URLs, bank account details, transaction references, receipts, emails, delivery records, and advertisements.
6. Report the scam to the platform where it happened, such as a marketplace, social media app, messaging app, payment app, or email provider.
7. Warn contacts if the scammer accessed the user's messaging, email, or social media account.
8. Watch for follow-up scams. Scammers may pretend to be police, lawyers, banks, recovery agents, or government officers and ask for fees or credentials.

## Singapore-specific contacts and references

- ScamShield: https://www.scamshield.gov.sg/i-have-been-scammed/
- ScamShield Helpline: 1799
- Singapore Police emergency assistance: 999
- Police Hotline for scam information: 1800-255-0000
- Online police report: https://eservices1.police.gov.sg/
- I-Witness e-Service: https://www.police.gov.sg/I-Witness

## If money was transferred

Tell the user to contact their bank immediately. They should give the bank transaction times, recipient names, account numbers, PayNow details, card details, platform names, and screenshots if available. Then they should file a police report with the same evidence.

Explain that recovery can be difficult, especially when funds are moved quickly or overseas. Avoid guaranteeing any refund.

## If personal information or OTP was shared

Tell the user to change passwords, revoke active sessions, enable two-factor authentication, contact the relevant bank or service provider, and monitor accounts. If Singpass or government services may be affected, advise them to use official channels only.

## If a suspicious app was installed

Tell the user to disconnect from the internet, avoid logging into banking or sensitive accounts on that device, uninstall the app if safe, run a security scan, and contact their bank from another trusted device. If banking activity may be affected, prioritise bank contact and police reporting.

## If an account was taken over

Tell the user to use the platform's account recovery flow from a trusted device, change the password, enable two-factor authentication, sign out other devices, warn contacts, and report the takeover to the platform.

## Good opening answer

"Act fast. First, stop replying and do not send more money or codes. If money or bank access is involved, call your bank now and ask them to block or freeze affected accounts. Then file a police report and keep screenshots, transaction details, phone numbers, links, and chat logs. If you are in Singapore and unsure, call the ScamShield Helpline at 1799. Tell me what happened: did you lose money, click a link, install an app, share an OTP, or lose access to an account?"
